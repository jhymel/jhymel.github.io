Technical Stuff

I am a chemistry graduate student at the Georgia Institute of Technology. I recieved my B.S. in Chemistry from the University of Tennessee in 2019, where I performed research under the advisement of Dr. Konstantinos Vogiatzis. More recently, I participated in the Higher Education Research Experience (HERE) program at the Nanomaterials Theory Institute at Oak Ridge National Laboratory under the direction of Dr. Bobby Sumpter.

My research interests as whole fall under the umbrella of computational chemistry and electronic structure theory, but I have worked on projects specifically involving CO2 capture, gas separations, and electron-beam/matter interactions, during which I have utilized methods such as classical molecular dynamics, ab initio molecular dynamics, density functional theory, and symmetry adapted perturbation theory.

The vast majority of my programming experience has involved writing Python and shell scripts to automate chemical calculations in HPC environments. With the heavy-lifing for these calculations being handled in C/C++/Fortran by software packages like Psi4, NWChem, and TurboMole.
